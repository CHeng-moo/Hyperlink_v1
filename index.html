✦ Story Begin — Bagworms and Black Holes

It started with a casual voice note.
"My latest obsession," my friend said, "is watching videos of wombats. They’re the cutest things ever."
I paused the audio. Wombats? I had never even heard of them. I thought it might be some kind of tiny animal — like a hamster or something.
So I did what anyone would do: I googled it.
I was just planning to check really quickly — maybe look at one photo, get the basic idea.
But the first thing I saw was this picture of a lady hugging an absolutely massive wombat. Like, sofa-sized. That was… unexpected.
Then I saw a related article headline that said: "Wombats poop cubes."
Excuse me?
I clicked.
Apparently yes, wombats are famous for their cube-shaped poop. There was a whole article explaining the biological mechanics of it, with diagrams.
I scrolled down, and of course — more links: “10 strangest animal digestive facts”, “Why capybaras are everyone’s best friend”, “Otters holding hands compilation”.
I don’t even remember which one I clicked next. Something soft and furry and blinking at the camera.
Fifteen minutes later I was watching cats knocking things off shelves. Wombats were long gone.

✦ A ROOM MADE OF LINKS

That’s when I realised... I was lost.
Not “where-am-I” kind of lost — more like“how-did-I-end-up-watching-this-pink-rabbit-eating-a-mini-pancake” kind of lost.
And the weirdest part? I started out just wanting to know what a wombat was.
But somewhere between the cube-shaped poop and the capybara friendship facts, I had been... hyperlink-ed.
It felt like I had opened a tiny door and fallen into a hallway of smaller, shinier doors — each one whispering, “click me, I’m just one more thing.”
Which, honestly, kind of worked.

Hyperlinks were meant to connect knowledge —
to let ideas flow freely across pages, forming a larger conversation.
But these days, more often than not, they seem to pull me away from what I was actually trying to focus on.

Sometimes I wonder if it’s really about curiosity…
or if I’ve just been gently, politely kidnapped by the internet.
It’s not even that I have no self-control (well, okay, maybe a little).
But there’s something about a blue underlined phrase that invites you to stray.
Like — are you even supposed to ignore it? It’s right there. Waiting. Sparkly.
And the thing is, you never really know where you’ll end up.
Maybe it's a helpful article.
Maybe it’s an ad pretending to be a helpful article.
Maybe it’s twenty more links.
Or maybe it’s a wombat again.

Who’s really choosing what I see, anyway?

✦ THIS IS PART OF IT TOO

That’s kind of why I made this page.
Not to stop the wandering — honestly, I think the wandering is the best part sometimes —
But to quietly see (and think about) how we wander.
How far we drift.
Whether we ever make it back to where we started…
or just keep clicking until it’s 2am and we’re reading about how snails sleep.
(Yes. That happened too.)

That’s pretty much the end of my little story — a few links, and the internet being its usual mischievous self.
Maybe you read it straight through.
Maybe you got completely lost, but somehow made it back.
Or maybe most people won’t even see this goodbye — lost somewhere along the way here.
That’s all part of the experiment.
Because honestly, I don’t think it’s just my reading that’s changed.
